Barriers Impacting Progress

Worsening / New concern Ongoing / Not resolved Improving

All barriers are sourced directly from IE Team observations across the three semi-annual reports.

👥
Policy Development Staffing & Capacity
Ongoing — All 3 Reports

Too few staff are tasked with the vast majority of policy revision work, creating bottlenecks and slow turnaround times. Key individuals are overburdened with multiple simultaneous assignments. While an external vendor was hired to add capacity, delays in returning drafts (e.g., 5+ months for Warrants policy, 6 weeks for FTO SOP) persist.

Impact: Directly delays all downstream work — training, accountability, and compliance assessments cannot begin until policies are approved.

Part 2 – Policy Part 5 – SSCA Part 7 – Training Part 10 – Accountability ⚠ Still unresolved as of Sept 2025
📁
UOF Case Review Backlog
Improving — but Funding Risk

As of September 2024, FIT had 1,111+ unreviewed use-of-force cases. By March 2025, the backlog was reduced by 72% overall (Level 1 down 89%), largely due to contracting an outside law firm for additional investigators. However, IE Team flagged the contract cap is nearly exhausted.

Risk: Without contract extension or permanent staffing additions, the backlog could rebound — especially since new UOF policies and training will likely increase future case volumes.

Part 4 – Use of Force Part 10 – Accountability ✓ Major progress made 🔴 Funding cap risk remains
🗂️
IA / OPCR Misconduct Investigation Backlog
Ongoing — Unevenly Improving

Both Internal Affairs and OPCR have historical backlogs of misconduct complaint investigations that have not yet been eliminated. OPCR showed marked improvement in timeliness and capacity by Report 3, but IA continues to lag. The new case management software intended to help is itself off-track.

Impact: Undermines officer accountability and damages community trust. Blocks Full and Effective Compliance for Part 10.

Part 10 – Accountability OPCR improving IA still lagging Case mgmt. system off-track
📋
SSCA Policy Delays — Blocking a Chain of Requirements
Ongoing — All 3 Reports

Stops, Searches, Citations & Arrests (SSCA) policies (9-100s, 9-200s, and Warrants 9-300s) remain unapproved over 2 years into the Agreement. Workshopping was paused in early 2025 to prioritize UOF. As of September 2025, the SSCA policies are still in informal feedback / workshopping phases.

Cascade effect: Delays SSCA training → delays Engaging with Minors policy (explicitly postponed to coordinate) → delays compliance assessments for Part 5 and related training under Part 7.

Part 5 – SSCA Part 7 – Training Engaging with Minors No paragraphs yet rated compliant
🏋️
Insufficient Training Delivery Capacity
Worsening — Identified Report 3

MPD's current training staff capacity is inadequate to meet the Department's ambitious Year 2 training goals. With multiple new policy curricula requiring delivery to the full department — Use of Force, NDP, BWC, Officer Wellness, Crisis Intervention, Supervisor Leadership, and more — the training pipeline is severely strained.

Impact: Training is the critical second step (after policy) before any paragraph can reach full Compliance. Delays in training delivery directly prevent compliance determinations across all Parts.

Part 7 – Training Part 3 – NDP Part 4 – UOF Part 6 – BWC Part 8 – Wellness 🔴 New concern in Report 3
🔗
Lack of Coordinated Leadership & Siloed Decision-Making
Emerging Concern — Report 3

Implementation of some requirements has been hindered by a lack of coordination between MPD and City units. IE Team observed siloed information and decision-making, key individuals overburdened with multiple tasks, and disciplinary decisions delayed due to multi-stakeholder administrative requirements.

Impact: Slows cross-departmental requirements (data systems, facilities, EIS, BCR co-response) and undermines consistent implementation across the organization.

Cross-cutting — all Parts Part 8 – Wellness / EIS Part 11 – Data 🔴 Named as key challenge in Report 3
📣
Failure to Communicate — Officer Misconceptions
Emerging Concern — Report 3

MPD focus groups and informal discussions revealed that officers continue to have significant misunderstandings and misconceptions about the Agreement and the IE Team's role. Officers perceive the required changes as being "forced on the Department" rather than as improvements MPD is committed to making.

Impact: Reduces officer buy-in, makes officer focus groups less productive, and may undermine effective implementation and accountability once policies are active.

Part 7 – Training Part 2 – Policy Organizational culture 🔴 Named as key challenge in Report 3
💊
Employee Support Plan — Staffing & Funding Gap
At Risk — Reports 2 & 3

The Employee Support Plan has been approved by the IE Team, but the City and MPD have not allocated adequate dedicated staffing or funding for implementation. As of March 2025, only two full-time personnel were assigned to the Wellness Unit — insufficient for the Plan's requirements. The Peer Support Team deployment also awaits full launch.

Impact: Year 2 implementation of the Employee Support Plan is flagged At Risk. Underfunding officer wellness undermines long-term sustainability of reform.

Part 8 – Officer Wellness 🔴 At Risk classification (Year 2)
💻
Technology & IT System Limitations
Ongoing — All 3 Reports

Current IT systems limit the effectiveness of compliance efforts across multiple Agreement requirements. Key technology projects remain off-track: the IA/OPCR misconduct case management system, the new UOF reporting module, and the EIS Phase 1 system. The policy management software (Policy and Resource Hub) was not yet live as of September 2025.

Impact: Without modernized systems, data collection for accountability is impaired and compliance auditing cannot proceed for several Part 10 and Part 11 requirements.

Part 10 – Accountability Part 11 – Data Part 8 – EIS IA/OPCR case mgmt. still off-track
🧠
Data Gaps Blocking Part 9 (Mental Health / BCR)
Persistent — All 3 Reports

The IE Team was unable to assess Part 9 requirements in Report 1 due to a complete lack of available baseline data. By Report 2, the City moved the Behavioral Crisis Response (BCR) program under the Fire Department and began assessing data gaps and operational deficiencies. Progress continued in Report 3, but Part 9 remains entirely "Not Yet Measured."

Impact: No compliance ratings can be issued for any Part 9 paragraphs until baseline data and operational assessments are complete.

Part 9 – Mental Health 0 paragraphs rated in all 3 reports
📜
FTO Program & Training Policy Gaps
Off-Track — Reports 2 & 3

The absence of an approved Field Training Officer (FTO) SOP continues to delay implementation of the FTO program. This is critical because new recruits who receive Academy training need that training reinforced through the FTO program once assigned to patrol. Training policies (2-500s) are also still in progress.

Impact: New officer training effectiveness is undermined, and the pipeline from Academy through field deployment is broken without a compliant FTO program.

Part 7 – Training Key concern in Report 3
📣
Community Oversight Engagement Challenges
Partially Addressed

In Report 1, the City's Community Commission on Police Oversight (CCPO) expressed frustration with MPD's policy feedback process: lack of notice, insufficient review time for voluminous policies, and lack of transparency about how community input was considered. MPD agreed to proactively notify CCPO when policies are posted. By Report 3, MPD published an extensive public accounting of how feedback was incorporated into UOF policies.

Status: Improving, but ongoing tension between Agreement timelines and community desire for meaningful input.

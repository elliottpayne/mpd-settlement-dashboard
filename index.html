Agreement Overview & Current Status

The Minneapolis Police Department Settlement Agreement, signed on July 13, 2023, between the City of Minneapolis and the Minnesota Department of Human Rights (MDHR), establishes a comprehensive reform framework across 11 substantive parts (Parts 2–11) spanning approximately 440 paragraphs. The Evaluation Plan spans 5 years (March 2024–March 2029), divided into three phases: Phase 1 (Foundational) focused on policy development and systems setup, Phase 2 (Implementation) emphasizing deployment and training delivery, and Phase 3 (Evaluation) dedicated to comprehensive audits and sustained compliance determination per Paragraph 440.

Training Requirements

Each officer must complete 152+ hours of training across 10 mandatory categories including Use of Force, Non-Discriminatory Policing, SSCA, Critical Decision-Making, Crisis Intervention, Body Worn Cameras, Officer Wellness, Implicit Bias, Community Engagement, and Leadership/Supervision.

2024 TNA Critical Gaps

  1. No master training schedule — ad hoc scheduling
  2. 40-hour FTO training not provided (SA required)
  3. Critical Decision-Making Model not integrated
  4. No adult learning principles — mostly lecture/PowerPoint
  5. Facility inadequate for scenario training
  6. No formal training evaluation process
  7. Poor Settlement Agreement communication to officers
  8. No supervisor training since 2022
  9. Training Division not analyzing critical incidents
  10. Training staffing shortages (512 sworn vs. 880 in 2019)
  11. No accountability for training attendance
  12. Insufficient scenario-based training
  13. No trainee feedback mechanism

12 Critical Barriers

1. Policy Staffing/Capacity Bottleneck
High
Too few policy writers; 5+ months for Warrants policy. Blocks downstream training and cascades delays across Parts 2, 5, 7, 10.
2. UOF Case Review Backlog Rebound Risk
High→Medium
72% backlog reduction via contract funding, now nearly exhausted. Risk of rebound unless permanent staffing secured.
3. IA/OPCR Misconduct Backlog
High
OPCR improving; IA lagging. Case management system off-track. Delays in misconduct resolution impact accountability.
4. SSCA Policy Delays (Cascade Blocker)
High (2+ years)
9-200 series (9-201–9-204) in community feedback since Jan 7, 2025; still Draft. 9-100s and Warrants similarly delayed. Blocks training delivery (began Feb 8, 2026, 6+ months late) and related policy approvals.
5. Insufficient Training Delivery Capacity
High
Training Division understaffed (512 sworn vs. 880 pre-2019). UOF delayed 6 weeks; SSCA delayed 6+ months; year 2 delivery at risk.
6. Lack of Coordinated Leadership
High (New)
Siloed information flows between policy, training, operations. Overburdened individuals. Delayed disciplinary decisions.
7. Officer Misconceptions About SA
Medium (New)
Officers perceive reforms as "forced." Unproductive focus group feedback; cultural resistance to training adoption.
8. Employee Support Plan Staffing Gap
High
Wellness Unit at 2 FTE (insufficient). Employee Support Plan approved but at-risk for sustained implementation.
9. IT/Technology & Facilities Limitations
Medium
Case management system off-track; Policy Hub delayed; EIS dependent on IT infrastructure not yet fully deployed. Needs assessments completed: Equipment & Technology Assessment (Jan 2024), Facility Condition Assessment (Jan 2024), Space Needs Assessment (Jan 2024). Response plan published June 2025 — implementation ongoing.
10. Data Gaps Blocking BCR
Medium
0 paragraphs rated across 3 reports. BCR moved to Fire Department; baseline metrics not established; inter-agency coordination barriers.
11. FTO SOP/Training Policy Gaps
Medium
4-hour FTO fundamentals started March 2025; 40-hour course Fall 2025. New recruit reinforcement not structured.
12. Community Oversight Engagement
Low→Medium
CCPO initial frustration addressed through proactive notifications and public accounting. Continued trust-building needed.

Glossary of Terms & Acronyms

ABLE
Active Bystandership for Law Enforcement — training program integrating peer intervention and ethical leadership principles.
ATP
Annual Training Plan — defined schedule of all required training courses for officers and supervisors.
BCR
Behavioral Crisis Response — alternative police response program for mental health crises; now administered by Fire Department.
BWC
Body Worn Camera — individual video camera worn by officers to record police-community interactions and use-of-force incidents.
CDM
Critical Decision-Making Model — core framework emphasizing sanctity of life and proportional response decision trees.
CIT
Crisis Intervention Team — specialized officers trained in de-escalation and mental health response protocols.
EIS
Early Intervention System — data-driven officer performance monitoring system identifying behavioral patterns and risk factors.
ELEFA
Effective Law Enforcement for All — the Independent Evaluator organization conducting compliance monitoring and semi-annual reporting.
FTO
Field Training Officer — experienced officer responsible for mentoring and evaluating new officers; requires 40-hour formal training per SA.
IA
Internal Affairs Division — MPD's internal investigative body responsible for misconduct investigations.
ICC
In-Car Camera — forward-facing and cabin video cameras installed in police vehicles to record community interactions and incidents.
MDHR
Minnesota Department of Human Rights — state civil rights agency; plaintiff/party to the Settlement Agreement.
MPD
Minneapolis Police Department — primary defendant and implementing agency for Settlement Agreement reforms.
NDP
Non-Discriminatory Policing — Part 3 of SA; requires policing practices free from racial, ethnic, and gender bias.
OPCR
Office of Police Conduct Review — civilian oversight body investigating citizen complaints and officer misconduct.
POST
Minnesota Peace Officer Standards and Training Board — state regulatory body setting law enforcement training and certification standards.
QRP
Quarterly Review Panels — accountability mechanism reviewing misconduct cases and recommending discipline (policy 2-601, approved Jan 2, 2026).
SSCA
Stops, Searches, Citations, and Arrests — Part 5 of SA; governs lawful, non-discriminatory police intervention authority.
TNA
Training Needs Assessment — annual analysis of training gaps, curriculum needs, and delivery capacity; informs Annual Training Plan (ATP).
UOF
Use of Force — Part 4 of SA; governs police application of physical force, weapons, and tactical options.
